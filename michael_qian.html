Minecraft invented

Minecraft was invented by Markus Persson, also known as "Notch," and the first version of the game, called "Minecraft Classic," was released on May 17, 2009. Inspired by games like Infiniminer, Dwarf Fortress, and Dungeon Keeper, Notch developed Minecraft as a sandbox game that emphasized creativity, exploration, and survival. Initially written in Java, the game allowed players to build and explore blocky, procedurally generated worlds.


GAINING POPULARITY

Minecraft began gaining significant popularity in 2010, during its alpha and beta phases, as word-of-mouth spread through online communities, gaming forums, and YouTube. Early adopters were drawn to its open-ended gameplay, creativity, and the ability to build and explore in a procedurally generated blocky world. The game's unique charm attracted players who enjoyed sandbox-style games, and its multiplayer mode helped communities form around servers.




MAJOR UPDATES

1.0 UPDATE

1.0 – Minecraft Release (November 18, 2011) Official release of Minecraft at MineCon 2011. Added The End dimension, featuring the Ender Dragon boss fight. Introduced brewing, enchanting, and hardcore mode. Added new mobs like Blazes, Magma Cubes, and Endermen.


1.1 UPDATE

1.1 – Minor Improvements (January 12, 2012) Spawn eggs added for creative mode. Introduced new languages, including Pirate Speak. Minor bug fixes and optimizations.



1.2 UPDATE

1.2.1 – Jungle Update (March 1, 2012) Added jungle biomes and jungle temples. Introduced ocelots (which could be tamed into cats). Added iron golems and climbing mechanics for vines. Expanded world height to 256 blocks.


1.3 UPDATE

1.3.1 – Adventure Update (August 1, 2012) Added emeralds and the trading system with villagers. Introduced adventure mode (for custom maps). Added desert temples, jungle temples, and books for writing.


1.4 UPDATE

1.4.2 – Pretty Scary Update (October 25, 2012) Introduced scary mobs like witches, bats, and the Wither boss. Added beacons, used to give boosts in a specific area. Decor-related blocks: item frames, anvils, and flower pots.


1.5 UPDATE

1.5 – Redstone Update (March 13, 2013) Focused on redstone mechanics: Added hoppers, droppers, comparators, and daylight sensors. Introduced quartz blocks and nether quartz ore.


1.6 UPDATE

1.6.1 – Horse Update (July 1, 2013) Added horses, donkeys, and mules, along with horse armor, leads, and name tags. Introduced the hay bale block.


1.7 UPDATE

1.7.2 – The Update That Changed the World (October 25, 2013) Major changes to world generation: Added new biomes like savannas, mesas, and roofed forests. Introduced flower variants and new types of trees (acacia and dark oak). Added stained glass and fishing loot.
